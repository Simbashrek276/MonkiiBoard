Clicky Switches Review

Audio & Tactile - Classic mechanical feel with distinctive click sound

Overview

Clicky switches are the quintessential mechanical keyboard experience, featuring both a tactile bump and an audible click sound. They provide maximum feedback through both physical and audio cues, making each keystroke satisfying and unmistakable.

Technical Specifications

Actuation Force

50-60g

Travel Distance

2.3mm actuation / 4.0mm total

Sound Level

Loud (5/5)

Lifespan

50-70 million presses

Performance Analysis

Typing Experience

Clicky switches deliver the most engaging typing experience:

  • Audible click provides instant confirmation of actuation
  • Tactile bump combined with audio feedback prevents bottoming out
  • Highly satisfying for touch typists who enjoy feedback
  • Encourages proper typing technique through clear actuation point
  • Rhythmic clicking can enhance typing speed for some users
üí° Pro Tip: If you love the feel but need quieter operation, consider "jailhouse modding" to reduce click volume.

Sound Profile

The signature click sound is both a feature and a consideration:

  • Distinct "click-clack" from click jacket mechanism
  • Can be heard across rooms - not suitable for quiet environments
  • Higher-pitched than tactile or linear switches
  • Sound profile varies between switch brands and types
  • Some find the sound therapeutic and satisfying
‚ö†Ô∏è Warning: Clicky switches may disturb others in shared spaces, libraries, or open offices. Consider your environment before choosing!

Gaming Performance

While possible, clicky switches have limitations for gaming:

  • Audio feedback can be useful for rhythm and timing games
  • Not ideal for voice chat - microphone will pick up clicks
  • Click mechanism adds slight resistance to rapid actuation
  • Better suited for turn-based or strategic games

Click Mechanisms

Different clicky switches use various click-generating mechanisms:

  • Click jacket: Metal leaf that snaps (Cherry MX Blue/Green)
  • Click bar: Shared metal bar (Cherry MX White)
  • Beam spring: Capacitive mechanism (IBM Model F)
  • Buckling spring: Spring buckles for click (IBM Model M)

Modding & Optimization

Jailhouse Modding

Insert small O-rings or clips to reduce click jacket travel, creating a quieter "jailhouse" switch with maintained tactility.

Spring Swapping

Replace springs to adjust actuation force without affecting click mechanism. Popular weights: 55g, 65g, 78g.

Lubrication

Lube housing rails and spring only. Avoid click mechanism to preserve sound. Use thin lube sparingly.

Foam Dampening

Add case foam and desk mat to reduce sound reverberation without eliminating the satisfying click.

Our Verdict

Clicky switches are the most polarizing switch type - you either love them or hate them. They provide unmatched feedback and the quintessential mechanical keyboard experience, but require consideration of your environment and those around you.
